Frequently Asked Questions

Answers to your most pressing questions.

Curated from our FAQ Friday series on social media. Have a question we haven't covered? Get in touch — we'll get back to you within one business day.

If your Comcare claim is assessed below the 10% Whole Person Impairment threshold, you have 60 days from the date of the determination to lodge an application with the Administrative Review Tribunal (ART). At Mannah Lawyers, we'll obtain comprehensive medico-legal evidence to challenge the assessment, prepare detailed submissions, and represent you at the Conciliation Conference where most matters are resolved without proceeding to a full hearing.

A "Medical Provider" encompasses a broad range of healthcare professionals and institutions. If you've been injured by negligent treatment from any of the following, you may have a valid claim:

Hospitals
Doctors
Surgeons
Nurses
Paramedics
Midwives
Dentists
Pharmacists
Medical Assistants
Radiologists
Lab Personnel
Cosmetologists

Determining whether a medical procedure has failed due to negligence requires careful investigation. We obtain your complete hospital and treatment records, then engage specialised independent doctors in the relevant field to provide expert opinions. These specialists assess whether your treatment deviated from the accepted medical standards a competent practitioner would have followed in the same circumstances. If the evidence shows a meaningful deviation that caused your injury, you likely have grounds to bring a claim.

Property occupiers owe a duty of care to anyone lawfully on their premises. If you've been injured at any of the following — and the owner failed to address a foreseeable hazard — you may be entitled to compensation:

Private homes
Retail stores and outlets
Office buildings
Construction sites
Government buildings

In our experience, approximately 90% of personal injury matters are resolved before reaching the courtroom — through conciliation, mediation, or direct negotiation with the insurer. Court proceedings are typically the last resort. That said, we always prepare every matter as if it will go to trial, because thorough preparation is what produces the strongest negotiating position. The insurer knows we'll go the distance — and that's exactly why most matters settle favourably long before trial.

A "duty of care" is a legal obligation to act with reasonable caution to avoid causing harm to others. Common examples include:

  • →A driver owes a duty to other road users to drive safely
  • →A business owes a duty to customers to keep premises safe
  • →An employer owes a duty to workers to provide a safe workplace
  • →A doctor owes a duty to patients to provide treatment to an accepted standard

The law doesn't demand perfection, but rather what is "reasonable" in the circumstances. When that standard is breached and causes injury, a claim may arise.

"No Win, No Fee" means exactly what it says — you only pay our professional fees if your claim is successful. There are no upfront costs, no monthly invoices, and no payment expected during the life of your matter. This arrangement removes the financial barrier to justice, ensuring that anyone with a valid claim can access experienced legal representation regardless of their financial circumstances. We'll explain the full costs agreement and any potential disbursements in detail at your free initial consultation, so you know exactly where you stand from day one.
